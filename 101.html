Astronomy 101

Embark on a cosmic journey exploring the wonders of our universe, from celestial bodies to space exploration.

How is the course structured?

This 7-week course takes you from basic astronomical concepts to advanced topics. The course is structured as follows:

Week 1

Solar System
Celestial Mechanics
Observational Tools

This week covers the basics of our solar system, fundamental celestial mechanics, and essential observational tools and techniques. You'll learn about planets, moons, and the motion of celestial bodies.

Week 2

Stars
Stellar Evolution
Astronomical Measurements

This week familiarizes you with stars, their classification, and stellar evolution. You'll learn about the life cycle of stars, from formation to various end states including white dwarfs, neutron stars, and black holes.

Week 3

Galaxies
Exoplanets
Deep Space Objects

This week covers galaxies, their formation and types, along with exoplanets and deep space objects like nebulae and star clusters. You'll learn how astronomers discover and characterize planets outside our solar system.

Week 4

Cosmology
Dark Matter
Dark Energy

This week covers cosmology and the large-scale structure of the universe. You'll explore mysteries like dark matter, dark energy, and the expanding universe. The Big Bang theory and the fate of the universe will be discussed.

Week 5

Space Exploration
Telescopes
Modern Astronomy

This week, you will start your journey into space exploration technologies and modern astronomical instruments. You'll learn about space missions, telescopes like James Webb and Hubble, and current research methodologies.
