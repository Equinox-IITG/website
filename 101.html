Astronomy 101

Embark on a cosmic journey exploring the wonders of our universe, from celestial bodies to space exploration.

How is the course structured?

This 4-week course takes you from basic astronomical concepts to advanced topics. The course is structured as follows:

Week 1

Early Universe
Dark Matter
Dark Energy

This week we'll discover the Big Bang story, learn about invisible dark forces shaping our universe, and explore cosmic mysteries. Plus, we'll introduce you to Python programming - the essential tool astronomers use for data analysis and research.

Week 2

Stars
Stellar Evolution
Linear Regression

This week we'll explore how stars are born, live, and die spectacularly - creating neutron stars and black holes along the way. We will also learn machine learning basics - how computers can find patterns in astronomical data automatically.

Week 3

Radio Astronomy
Spectroscopy
Observational Tools

This week familarizes you with telescopes beyond what your eyes see and gravitational waves. We shall also learn linear regression - a powerful method to find relationships between different cosmic measurements.

Week 4

Cosmic Diversity
Celestial Identification
Classification Models

This week covers different cosmic objects and explore the biggest unsolved mysteries astronomers face today. We will learn in depth about classification algorithms - letting computers automatically sort stars, galaxies, and planets from observational data.

Project

Final Project
Presentation
Peer Review

This is dedicated to completing and presenting your first astronomy project in the club. You'll demonstrate mastery of course concepts through team research and analysis, culminating in a comprehensive presentation to the club.
