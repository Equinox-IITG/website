Our Projects

Explore our ongoing research endeavors, educational initiatives, and community outreach programs that bring the wonders of the universe closer to everyone at IIT Guwahati and beyond.

Active

IITG Radio Telescope Array

Building a small-scale radio telescope array to detect solar flares and map radio sources in our galaxy. Using software-defined radio technology to process astronomical signals.

Coordinator: Dr. Ananya Sharma Learn More
Active

Exoplanet Transit Observation

Using our campus observatory to detect exoplanet transits and contribute data to international databases. Students learn photometry techniques and data analysis.

Coordinator: Prof. Rajiv Mehta Learn More
Completed

Meteor Shower Monitoring Network

A network of automated cameras across campus to detect and analyze meteor activity. Successfully documented the 2024 Perseid meteor shower with over 300 captures.

Coordinator: Dr. Mihir Patel View Results
Active

Portable Planetarium Outreach

Bringing astronomy to local schools with our portable planetarium. Club members develop and present educational programs for K-12 students in the Guwahati region.

Coordinator: Priya Desai Learn More
Upcoming

Solar Observatory Installation

Developing a dedicated solar observation platform with H-alpha filters to monitor solar activity, prominences, and sunspots. Will include public viewing opportunities.

Coordinator: Arjun Singh Get Involved
Active

Campus Light Pollution Survey

Mapping light pollution across campus and surrounding areas to identify opportunities for better astronomical viewing and energy-efficient lighting solutions.

Coordinator: Neha Gupta View Map
